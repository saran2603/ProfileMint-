Why it matters

The invisible interview
happens before you arrive.

Your resume and portfolio are working — or failing — on your behalf right now. Here is what the data says about why this matters more than most students realise.

7s

The 7-Second Verdict

Research shows recruiters spend an average of 6–7 seconds scanning a resume before deciding whether to continue. That's not enough time to read — it's only enough time to notice. Optimized structure makes those seconds count.

75%

ATS Filters First

75% of resumes are automatically rejected by Applicant Tracking Systems before any human sees them. Not because the candidate was weak — because the document wasn't formatted to pass. We fix that.

1st

Your First Job Defines Your Story

The first role shapes your LinkedIn profile, your reference network, and the story you tell for the next five years. Getting it right isn't just about one job — it's about the trajectory that follows.

3×

Portfolio = Visible Proof

Candidates with portfolios receive 3× more recruiter engagement. In a world where everyone claims skills on a resume, a portfolio shows them. For designers, developers, MBAs and analysts — it's no longer optional.

Clarity Is the Competitive Edge

Most resumes are dense, unfocused, or formatted for the wrong audience. When your resume is clear, recruiters don't have to work to understand your value — and the ones who don't make them work are the ones who get called.

The Compounding Effect

A strong resume gets you the interview. A strong portfolio builds credibility before you speak. Together they create a compounding impression — one that positions you as someone who takes their career seriously, even as a fresher.

What's holding you back

The problems most
freshers don't know they have.

01

Sending into silence

Dozens of applications, zero responses. The resume looks fine to you — but recruiters are skipping it in seconds. The issue isn't your profile, it's the packaging.

02

The template trap

When everyone uses the same Canva or MS Word format, nothing stands out. A generic template signals generic effort — even when the experience isn't generic at all.

03

The ATS wall

Most companies screen resumes with software before a human ever sees them. Wrong keywords, wrong formatting, wrong file structure — and you're out before you're even in.

04

Duties, not impact

"Responsible for social media management" tells a recruiter nothing. "Grew Instagram engagement by 40% in 3 months" tells them everything. Most freshers never learn this difference.
